Overview

Lonzo Anderson Ball is an American professional basketball player and he currently plays for the Los Angeles Lakers of the NBA. He is a 6' 6" point guard and his jersey number is 2. In college, he played for the UCLA Bruins for one season. The highest scoring game in his rookie season was against the Phoenix Suns on October 20, 2017 (29 Pts, 11 Reb, 9 Ast). The Lakers selected him with the second overall pick of the 2017 NBA draft.

Early Life

Lonzo Ball was born on October 27, 1997, in Anaheim, California to LaVar and Tina Ball. His parents were both former college basketball players. LaVar also played American football professionally. Lonzo Ball started playing basketball at the age of two. Ball idolized Lebron James since he was young. He played on a team coached by his father with his younger brothers, LiAngelo and LaMelo Ball, until high school. He played basketball at Chino Hills High School in Chino Hills, California. He averaged 25 points, 11 rebounds, 9.1 assists, 5 blocks, and 5 steals as a junior. His two younger brothers and his cousin, Andre were also on the team. He received many national honours.

College

Lonzo Ball went to the University of California, Los Angeles(UCLA) and played for the Bruins. He was rated a five-star recruit by major scouting services. His vision and passing skills had helped UCLA rise on the national rankings. In the 2017 NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Tournament, UCLA's overall rank was 11. For the season, Ball averaged 14.6 points, 7.6 assists, and 6.0 rebounds.

NBA career

Lonzo Ball was selected with the 2nd overall draft pick of the 2017 NBA draft. Magic Johnson, the president of basketball operations for the Lakers, billed Lonzo as "the new face of the Lakers." Lonzo Ball was named the league MVP during the 2017 NBA Summer League in Las Vegas. He had limited playing time in his rookie season due to his injuries. Breaking Lebron James' record of being the youngest player to achieve a triple-double by five days, Ball became the youngest player to achieve a triple-double at the age of 20 years and 15 days. In the 2018-2019 season, Lonzo went through a surgery which limited his playing time, but he had increased his strength, mostly in his upper body during the time he wasn't playing. This had improved his durability which made him a better defender. Lonzo Ball was selected for the Rising Stars game but was ruled out again because of an ankle injury he had gotten during the game against the Rockets on January 19, 2019. Big Baller Brand

Big Baller Brand (BBB) is an American company that designs, manufactures, and sells clothing and shoes. It belongs to Lonzo Ball's father, LaVar Ball and it makes signature shoes for the three Ball brothers, Lonzo, LaMelo, and LiAngelo Ball. Zo2's for Lonzo, Melo Ball 1's for LaMelo, and Gelo 3's for LiAngelo. Big Baller Brand was launched in 2016. LaVar said that he created Big Baller Brand for his sons and that each of the B's on the company's logo stands for one of his sons. Big Baller Brand was given an F grade by the Better Business Bureau in January 2018 because of its pattern of complaints. Many people had to wait many weeks or even months to receive their orders. Some customers say that they have received the wrong merchandise or even nothing at all. Most of their items are also very expensive, such as the ZO2 Prime Remix shoes which are $495.00.
