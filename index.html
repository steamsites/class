STEAM Education

STEAM is a new and exciting movement in education that meets the needs of a 21st century economy. The acronym stands for Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts and math and encomposes most of the key components that many employers, educators, and parents feel as important for students to thrive in the present and rapidly approaching future.

STEM vs. STEAM

In recent years, there has been much talk about incorporating STEM programs in our schools. The reasons being that our students will be able to take on the future job market, which will mainly contain jobs in science, technology, engineering and math feilds. Therefore, there has been an increased investment in STEM initiatives in schools such as robotics programs, coding clubs, hands-on exploration, and stem based projects. However, many people elt that the critical process os creativity and innovation was missing from STEM learning initiatives. The new thought was that STEM programs may have more experiential learning opportunitites, but they are limited to only science, technology, engineering and math. Our future jobs would require so much more than just understanding these areas--it requires applicaiton, creation and ingenuity.


This is where STEAM comes in. STEAM takes STEM education to the next level. It simply allows students to connect their learning in these critical areas with arts practices. STEAM is said to remove limitations and replaces them with wonder, critique, inquiry, and innovation.


We have been doing many exciting activities and projects in STEAM. One of our most challenging and fun projects was learning HTML and CSS to make our own websites. Students were introduced to the html and css programming basics and they then started to build thier own websites.
